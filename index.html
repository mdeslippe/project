Overview

The first workable prototype of the Internet came in the late 1960s with the creation of ARPANET, or the Advanced Research Projects Agency Network. Originally funded by the U.S. Department of Defense, ARPANET used packet switching to allow multiple computers to communicate on a single network (Andrews, 2019). The technology continued to grow in the 1970s after scientists Robert Kahn and Vinton Cerf developed Transmission Control Protocol and Internet Protocol, or TCP/IP, a communications model that set standards for how data could be transmitted between multiple networks (Andrews, 2019). Many regard the creation of the TCP/IP model as the birth of the internet, as networks could now be interconnected with one another.

For many years, those who worked in specialized fields or were a part of academic circles were the only ones familiar with the internet. The invention of the World Wide Web (WWW) is what really allowed it to take off. Tim Berners-Lee, a researcher at the European Council for Nuclear Research, formalised a proposal for the World Wide Web in November 1990. It outlined the principal concepts and it defined important terms behind the Web. Shortly after, had the prgrammed the first Web server and browser, and had it up and running (CERN).

Fast-forward to the modern day, and the internet powers the world. From communication to commerce, to education, to entertainment, the internet has revolutionized how we live out lives. Social media platforms connect billions of people across the globe, enabling instant sharing of information and ideas. E-commerce platforms allow consumers to shop from the comfort of their homes, with products delivered right to their doorstep. The internet's impact on society continues to grow, and continues to shape the way we live our lives.

Key Milestones

  • Development of ARPANET by the U.S. Department of Defense.
  • Standardization of the Internet Protocol Suite (TCP/IP).
  • Introduction of email and dns services.
  • Introduction of web browsers and websites.
  • Introduction of broadband and mobile internet services.
  • Emergence of social media platforms like Facebook and Twitter.
  • Rapid expansion of online streaming services for entertainment.
  • Advancements in the Internet of Things (IoT).
