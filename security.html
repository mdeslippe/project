Data Breaches

A data breach is defined as any security incident in which unauthorized parties gain access to sensitive or confidential information, including personal data including Social Security numbers, bank account numbers, healthcare data or corporate data including customer data records, intellectual property, and financial information (IBM, 2023). Individuals often have little to no say in how their data is kept secure. They are forced to rely on organizations to handle this aspect, even though they're the ones who suffer the most from data breaches (Deslippe, 2024). While you may not have direct control over how companies manage your data, you can reduce the data you share with them. By providing only essential personal information to organizations, any potential data breach would result in minimal information being stolen.

Phishing

The term phishing attack refers to an attempt to steal sensitive information, typically in the form of usernames, passwords, credit card numbers, bank account information or other important data in order to utilize or sell the stolen information. By masquerading as a reputable source with an enticing request, an attacker lures in the victim in order to trick them, similarly to how a fisherman uses bait to catch a fish (Cloudflare). Phishing attacks are often carried out through deceptive emails, fraudulent websites, or other forms of communication that are designed to appear legitimate. To protect yourself from phishing attacks, always stop and double check the legitimacy of the request for information. This can be as simple as checking to make sure the website url, or email address is correct. Check for simple spelling mistakes or grammar errors that reputable sources would not make.

Computer Viruses

A computer virus is a type of malicious code or program written to alter the way a computer operates and that is designed to spread from one computer to another. A virus operates by inserting or attaching itself to a legitimate program or document that supports macros in order to execute its code. In the process a virus has the potential to cause unexpected or damaging effects, such as harming the system software by corrupting or destroying data (Norton Canada, 2018). The are a large variety virus types, some of the most common include: file-infecting viruses, macro viruses, web browser Viruses, and boot sector viruses (Shield Services, 2023). To keep yourself safe from computer viruses, you can apply the following security measures: enable anti-virus software, update your software to the latest version, use multi-factor authentication, use an ad-blocker when navigating the web, be aware of the signs of phishing, and always think before you click (Government of Canada, 2023).
